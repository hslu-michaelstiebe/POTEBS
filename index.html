SFOE Research Project

Investigating the Potential of E-Bike Sharing for Sustainable Mobility

POTEBS compares two e-bike sharing systems across urban, peri-urban, and rural contexts in the Basel metropolitan area — to understand who uses them, how, and what this means for transport policy.

About the Project

Two Systems, One Region, Many Questions

The Basel metropolitan area hosts two concurrent e-bike sharing systems with fundamentally different designs — creating a natural experiment for studying shared micromobility.

Pick-e-Bike

Free-floating
  • E-bike class: S-Pedelec (up to 45 km/h)
  • Fleet: ~500 e-bikes + 150 e-scooters
  • Coverage: Broader agglomeration
  • Archetype: Flexibility maximizers

PubliBike / Velospot

Dock-based
  • E-bike class: Conventional (25 km/h)
  • Infrastructure: Fixed docking stations
  • Coverage: Urban core Basel
  • Archetype: Routine optimizers
Study Area

The Basel Metropolitan Area

The two systems serve overlapping but distinct geographies — from the dense urban core to peri-urban communities in the Birsstadt and Leimental.

Research Focus

What We Study

User Segmentation

Identifying distinct usage archetypes through exploratory factor analysis and Gaussian mixture models.

Usage Intensity Drivers

Examining attitudes, weather resilience, and multimodal integration via multinomial logit models.

Spatial Differentiation

How do urban, peri-urban, and rural contexts shape EBSS demand and usage patterns?

Modal Substitution

Which transport modes does EBSS replace — and what does this mean for sustainability claims?

Pragmatism, not ideology, drives intensive e-bike sharing use.

Our flagship paper finds that pro-environmental self-identity negatively predicts intensive usage — while weather resilience and multimodal integration are the strongest drivers.
