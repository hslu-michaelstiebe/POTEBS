02 · Findings

What the results show.

I.

Extreme concentration and near-50% dormancy.

Across both systems, a minority of riders accounts for the overwhelming majority of trips. Almost half of registered users never complete a single journey — the Gini coefficient of usage sits at 0.77.

Gini 0.77
II.

Pro-environmental self-identity reduces power-user likelihood.

Counter to the dominant narrative, environmental attitude is not a strong positive predictor of intensive use. Power users are primarily pragmatic utility-optimizers.

Against the narrative
III.

EBSS primarily substitutes public transport, not cars.

The mode-shift pattern is closer to tram-and-bus complement than to car-replacement — which has direct implications for how impact is reported and how operators should communicate value.

Policy implication
“Electric bike sharing appears useful in a real but bounded way.”
— POTEBS, interim finding
